French protectorate period 1881 - 1956

After its occupation of Algeria in 1830, France imposed harsh economic treaties on the Tunisian countries, which were already exhausted from successive crises despite attempts to reform, and then France took advantage of the weakness that engulfed the body of the Ottoman Empire and invaded Tunisian soil in the fateful year of Tunisia 1881. Tunisia was at that time The subject of a conflict between the European colonial powers, especially Italy and England, as well as France, the invading forces met fierce resistance from the people, especially the tribesmen, but the lack of numbers and equipment compared to the French regular army led to the defeat of the Tunisians and forced the Bey to sign a humiliating treaty for Tunisia, according to which France imposed protection On Tunisia, it was called the Treaty of Protection on May 2, 1881 . The bey was forced to sign a humiliating treaty for Tunisia, under which France imposed protection on Tunisia and was called the Treaty of Protection on May 2, 1881, and the French protection continued until 1956, when the full independence protocol was signed between Tunisia and France.

Independence

The global situation after the Second World War (1939-1945), which was characterized by bipolarity (the Cold War), helped the New Constitutional Free Party to consolidate its position on the Walesian points, especially the right of peoples to self-determination against France, which had become a weak colonial power retreating from the war like England. Although France promised the party independence in 1952, it broke the promise, which resulted in the armed revolution in 1954, which made France go to negotiations in 1955, in which the government of Mendes France approved internal independence, which was opposed by Saleh Ben Youssef, who considered it a step backward and then complete independence in 1956, which saw some opposition from the far right and supporters of France.
